# NutriCare Recipes - User Guide

## 🎯 Quick Start

### Step 1: Open the Application
Open `index.html` in your web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge - any modern browser works)

### Step 2: Select Your Health Condition
- On the home page, you'll see different health condition cards
- Click the **"Select"** button under **"Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD)"**
- You'll be taken to the recipes page

### Step 3: Browse Recipes
- You'll see the cuisine selector with three options:
  - 🇨🇳 **Chinese** - Asian-inspired recipes
  - 🇺🇸 **Western** - Traditional Western cuisine
  - 🇹🇭 **Thai** - Thai and Southeast Asian dishes

### Step 4: Find Your Recipe
Use the search and filter options to narrow down recipes:

#### Search Box
- Type recipe names (e.g., "Steamed Fish", "Chicken Stir-Fry")
- Search for ingredients (e.g., "garlic", "broccoli")
- Results update as you type

#### Meal Type Filter
- **Breakfast** - Morning options with lighter proteins
- **Lunch** - Balanced midday meals
- **Dinner** - Heartier evening dishes
- **Snack** - Light, complementary options
- **Dessert** - Renal-friendly sweet treats

#### Preparation Time Filter
- **Quick (<30 min)** - Fast weeknight meals
- **Medium (30-60 min)** - Standard cooking time
- **Longer (>60 min)** - Special occasion dishes

### Step 5: View Recipe Details
- Click the **"View Recipe"** button on any recipe card
- A popup will appear showing:
  - Full ingredient list
  - Step-by-step instructions
  - Complete nutritional information
  - Renal health notes

### Step 6: Take Action
- **Read the instructions carefully**
- **Review nutritional values** to verify portions
- **Check the renal health note** for special considerations
- **Close the popup** by clicking the ✕ button
- **Consult your healthcare provider** before making diet changes

## 📊 Understanding Nutritional Information

When you open a recipe detail, you'll see:

### Nutritional Values Explained

#### **Calories**
- How much energy the recipe provides
- Typical range: 150-350 calories per serving
- Balances carbs, protein, and fat

#### **Potassium (K)**
- **Why it matters:** Too much can affect heart rhythm
- **Goal for CKD patients:** <300mg per serving
- **Foods high in K:** Bananas, tomatoes, spinach
- **Foods low in K:** Cabbage, green beans, white rice
- *Tips:* Draining vegetables reduces potassium by 30%

#### **Phosphorus (P)**
- **Why it matters:** Too much causes bone disease
- **Goal for CKD patients:** <150mg per serving
- **Foods high in P:** Meat, dairy, nuts, whole grains
- **Foods low in P:** White rice, plain vegetables
- *Tips:* Limiting meat portions is key

#### **Sodium (Na)**
- **Why it matters:** Too much raises blood pressure
- **Goal for renal patients:** <85mg per serving
- **Foods high in Na:** Processed foods, canned goods, salt
- **Foods low in Na:** Fresh foods, home-cooked meals
- *Tips:* Use herbs and spices instead of salt

#### **Protein**
- **Why it matters:** Essential for body function, but kidneys need rest
- **Goal stage 3:** 25-30g per day
- **Goal stage 4-5:** 20-25g per day
- **Per serving:** Usually 15-35g in our recipes
- *Tip:* Balance meat with vegetables

#### **Fiber**
- **Why it matters:** Supports digestive and heart health
- **Goal:** 25-35g per day from all foods
- **Serves as:** Bonus health benefit from vegetables

## 🥗 Using Recipes Effectively

### Step-by-Step:

1. **Read Ingredients First**
   - Do you have all ingredients?
   - Note any substitutions needed
   - Check expiration dates

2. **Review Instructions**
   - Read completely before starting
   - Note equipment needed
   - Gather all items (mise en place)

3. **Check Renal Notes**
   - Understand why it's kidney-friendly
   - Note portion recommendations
   - Check any warnings

4. **Prepare**
   - Wash and chop vegetables
   - Measure proteins carefully
   - Get cooking tools ready

5. **Cook**
   - Follow steps in order
   - Use low-sodium seasonings
   - Adjust timing as needed

6. **Serve**
   - Plate appropriate portion size
   - Store leftovers properly in fridge
   - Portion for future meals if making extra

## 💡 Tips for Success

### Portion Control
- **Use a scale** for accurate protein portions
- **Measure vegetables** to stay within limits
- **Use smaller plates** to make portions look adequate
- **Reference nutrition info** for serving sizes

### Flavor Without Salt
- Use **fresh herbs:** dill, parsley, cilantro, basil
- Add **spices:** garlic, ginger, black pepper, turmeric
- Try **acids:** lemon juice, lime juice, vinegar
- Consider **low-sodium sauces** (check labels!)

### Meal Planning
- **Plan 1 week** at a time
- **Vary cuisines** for interest (Mon Chinese, Wed Thai, Fri Western)
- **Mix easy and complex** recipes
- **Prep ingredients ahead** on Sundays

### Shopping Smart
- **Buy fresh** when possible
- **Read nutrition labels** carefully
- **Call dietitian** before buying new products
- **Frozen vegetables** are fine (no added sodium)

### Cooking Techniques
- **Steaming:** Preserves nutrients, minimal salt
- **Baking:** Prevents oil absorption
- **Grilling:** Natural char adds flavor
- **Boiling:** Reduces potassium 30-50% (drain liquid)
- **Stir-frying:** Quick, minimal oil, full flavor

## ⚠️ Important Safety Notes

### Before You Start
- **Consult your doctor** before making any diet changes
- **Talk to a renal dietitian** for personalized guidance
- **Check with your healthcare team** on portion sizes
- **Don't change diet suddenly** - make gradual transitions

### When Cooking
- **Verify ingredients** for hidden sodium or potassium
- **Use fresh herbs** instead of salt-containing seasonings
- **Drain liquids** when boiling vegetables
- **Control portions** carefully
- **Avoid substitutions** without checking nutrition

### Storage & Safety
- **Refrigerate immediately** after cooking
- **Use within 3-4 days** 
- **Freeze extra portions** in labeled containers
- **Thaw properly** (not at room temperature)
- **Reheat to 165°F** internal temperature

## 🚨 When to Contact Your Doctor

Stop eating the recipe and contact your provider if:
- You experience **dizziness or shortness of breath**
- You have **swelling in legs or hands**
- You notice **weight gain over 2-3 pounds**
- You feel **unusual fatigue**
- You have **difficulty urinating**

## 🔄 Frequently Asked Questions

### Q: Can I modify recipes?
**A:** Yes! You can:
- Substitute similar vegetables
- Use different protein types (check nutrition)
- Adjust herbs and spices
- Change cooking methods
- Always verify nutrition values if changing significantly

### Q: What if I don't like an ingredient?
**A:** Options:
- Skip it (won't hurt the dish much)
- Substitute similar low-potassium vegetable
- Use different herbs for flavor
- Ask your dietitian for alternatives

### Q: Can I double the recipe?
**A:** Yes, but:
- Calculate nutrition × number of servings
- Adjust cooking time (may need longer)
- Still follow portion recommendations
- Freeze extra portions

### Q: Are portions flexible?
**A:** Somewhat:
- Ask your dietitian for YOUR specific portions
- CKD stage determines exact amounts
- Generally no more than 3-4 oz protein
- Track your weights and symptoms

### Q: Can I eat these recipes daily?
**A:** Most yes:
- Rotating 2-3 recipes daily is healthy
- Variety keeps diet interesting
- Monitor your labs regularly
- Adjust portions if needed

### Q: How do I know if it's working?
**A:** Track:
- **Weight** (weigh daily)
- **Lab values** (potassium, phosphorus, creatinine)
- **How you feel** (energy, swelling, symptoms)
- **Blood pressure** (if recommended)
